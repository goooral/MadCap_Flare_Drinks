Introduction

Fermentation, an ancient process harnessed by cultures worldwide, is the magic behind transforming simple ingredients into complex and flavorful beverages. It involves the conversion of sugars into alcohol or acids through the action of microorganisms like yeast and bacteria.

Fermented drinks, from ancient brews to modern craft concoctions, have been cherished for centuries. They offer a diverse range of flavors, from the tartness of kombucha to the richness of wine. Beyond their taste, fermented drinks often contain probiotics, beneficial bacteria that support gut health and overall well-being.

An almost unlimited supply of made-to-measure carbonated drinks can easily be produced at home, using tools and ingredients available in most households. This guide serves as a short introduction into this wonderful craft.
